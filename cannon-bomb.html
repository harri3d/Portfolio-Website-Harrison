'Cheating' at Shockwaves

The shockwave was a fun puzzle to solve. How could I explode all those little coins and splinters?

At first, I thought explosion simulations must use things like air pressure. However, I realized there's usually no need. Simply giving each piece its own random velocity and activating them with a growing sphere makes them jump away from the center on their own! They're very obedient wood splinters.

This is part of what I love about Houdini. The "physically accurate" method is not always the best approach. The sim would be hard to control and painfully slow if I were to use air pressure. But with a few lines of VEX, I can "cheat" and have much more precise control, a faster sim, and all the little pieces acting on cue.
