Cannon-Bomb

It's a cannonball, it's a bomb. Need I say more?

Every FX reel needs an explosion. Since I had to do it, I wondered, how can I do it in an interesting way? Inspired by the slapstick humour of the cartoons I grew up with, I originally imagined a cannonball crashing into a pirate ship, and of course igniting a container of ACME TNT.

If I need to communicate an idea in a fraction of a second, it forces me to make the visual language as clear as possible. I realized it would be impossible to communicate so many ideas, so I combined them to make the cannon-bomb, perhaps it was sent by some really nasty pirates. I set my scene in the hold of the ship, to give me an opportunity to flex my RBD simulation skills, as the explosion strew around all the pirate treasure. Plus, I'd only need to worry about the ship's interior, and with a limited view of the open ocean, it's less work for me to do!
